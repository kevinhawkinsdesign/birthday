The Plan

A week of everything

We'll be based at Masia Can Brull with days full of good food, open air, and whatever mood strikes.

🥾

Hiking

Montseny Natural Park is right on our doorstep — trails through oak and beech forests, waterfalls, and sweeping views of the Osona valley. All levels welcome.

🦌

Hunting

The mountains around Brull are classic hunting country. Whether you're an old hand or a first-timer, we'll have days out in the field to look forward to.

🏊

Swimming

Cool off in the pool with cold drinks, good conversation, and zero agenda. The height of an August afternoon in Catalonia.

🍷

Wine Tastings

Catalonia produces world-class wine and cava. We'll explore nearby bodegas and enjoy long lunches with bottles of something excellent from the region.

⛳

Golf

Club de Golf Montanya is minutes from the masia — a scenic 18-hole course designed by David Thomas, set in the hills with stunning mountain backdrops.

🌑

Total Solar Eclipse

On August 12, a total solar eclipse crosses Spain at sunset. Drive ~2 hrs to Tarragona for totality, or watch a stunning deep partial eclipse from the masia. One of nature's greatest spectacles — and it's happening right on our doorstep.

🪵

Just Chilling

Long dinners on the terrace. Evening fires. Slow mornings with coffee and nowhere to be. The best kind of week.

Getting There

Travel Guide

Fly into Barcelona and drive up through the mountains. It's one of the better airport-to-destination journeys in Europe.

Step OneFly to Barcelona BCN

Book into Barcelona El Prat Airport (BCN) — the main international hub, 50km south of El Brull.

Aim to land on August 10 with enough time to collect your rental car and reach the hotel before evening.

Departing on August 16? Allow 2+ hours from El Brull to the airport.

Book flights as early as possible — August is peak season and fares rise sharply.

Step TwoRent a Car at the Airport

🚗  We strongly recommend renting a car at BCN. El Brull is a mountain village — a car gives you total freedom for the whole week.

All major rental agencies are at BCN Terminal 1 & T2: Hertz, Europcar, Avis, SIXT, Enterprise.

The drive to El Brull is around 1 hour via the C-17 motorway heading north toward Vic. Scenic and easy.

Consider splitting a car with others arriving on the same flight to save on costs.

Good to KnowPractical Tips

Weather: El Brull sits at ~800m — August days are warm (24–30°C) but evenings cool down nicely. Bring a light layer.

Visa: Spain is Schengen. EU/EEA passports enter freely. Others check requirements for Spain before travelling.

Currency: Euro (€). Cards accepted widely; bring a little cash for smaller villages and markets.

Data: Consider an EU/Spain eSIM — Airalo or Holafly offer affordable plans. Coverage in Brull can be variable.

Language: Catalan and Spanish both spoken. English works fine in most places.
