I just want to be with you. That's the whole plan.

The Plan

A week of everything

We'll be based at Masia Can Brull with days full of good food, open air, and whatever mood strikes.

🥾

Hiking

Montseny Natural Park is right on our doorstep — trails through oak and beech forests, waterfalls, and sweeping views of the Osona valley. All levels welcome.

🦌

Hunting

The mountains around Brull are classic hunting country. Whether you're an old hand or a first-timer, we'll have days out in the field to look forward to.

🏊

Swimming

Cool off in the pool with cold drinks, good conversation, and zero agenda. The height of an August afternoon in Catalonia.

🍷

Wine Tastings

Two exceptional bodegas are right on our doorstep. Celler Sant Miquel d'Oló — a family winery at 800m with a Romanesque hermitage and tastings of 4 wines with local products. And Les Acàcies in Avinyó — claimed to be the smallest winery in the world, guided vineyard tour with cheese, sausages and pa amb tomàquet for €15/person.

⛳

Golf

Club de Golf Montanya is minutes from the masia — a scenic 18-hole course designed by David Thomas, set in the hills with stunning mountain backdrops. 9 holes €100 · 18 holes €140.

🌑

Total Solar Eclipse

On August 12, a total solar eclipse crosses Spain at sunset. Drive ~2 hrs to Tarragona for totality, or watch a stunning deep partial eclipse from the masia. One of nature's greatest spectacles — and it's happening right on our doorstep.

🏺

Pottery

Get your hands in the clay. A grounding, unhurried activity that fits perfectly with the pace of the week — and you leave with something you actually made.

🪵

Just Chilling

Long dinners on the terrace. Evening fires. Slow mornings with coffee and nowhere to be. The best kind of week.

The Natural Park

Montseny Natural Park

One of Europe's most biodiverse protected areas, and our backyard for the week. The masia sits within the reserve, on 200 acres of its own land.

31,000+ Hectares of park
1978 UNESCO Biosphere Reserve
270+ Vertebrate species
600+ Plant species

Montseny sits 50km north of Barcelona in the Catalan Pre-Coastal Range, straddling the districts of Osona, La Selva, and Vallès Oriental. Its range of altitude — from valley floors to the 1,706m summit of Turó de l'Home — creates a remarkable layering of ecosystems: holm oak and cork oak on the lower slopes, pine forests through the middle, and beech and silver fir closer to the peaks.

That natural fir forest is the southernmost in Europe, and the main reason UNESCO declared the area a Biosphere Reserve in 1978. The reserve was later extended to cover more than 50,000 hectares.

For us, it's a backdrop for morning hikes, evening fires, and the kind of stillness that's increasingly hard to find. The masia's own 200 acres give us a private slice of it all.

Wildlife in the park

  • Wild boar, badger and grey dormouse roam the forest floor
  • Woodcock, bullfinch and other Central European birds nest here
  • The Montseny brook newt — found nowhere else on Earth
  • Pyrenean newt, grass frog and Aesculapian snake in the streams
  • Mediterranean and Central European fauna communities overlapping — a rare convergence
  • And of course, the three dogs at Can Brull, who know every trail

Getting There

Travel Guide

Fly into Barcelona and drive up through the mountains. It's one of the better airport-to-destination journeys in Europe.

Step OneFly to Barcelona BCN

Book into Barcelona El Prat Airport (BCN) — the main international hub, 50km south of El Brull.

Aim to land on August 11 with enough time to collect your rental car and reach the hotel before evening.

Departing on August 16? Allow 2+ hours from El Brull to the airport.

Book flights as early as possible — August is peak season and fares rise sharply.

Step TwoRent a Car at the Airport

🚗  We strongly recommend renting a car at BCN. El Brull is a mountain village — a car gives you total freedom for the whole week.

All major rental agencies are at BCN Terminal 1 & T2: Hertz, Europcar, Avis, SIXT, Enterprise.

The drive to El Brull is around 1 hour via the C-17 motorway heading north toward Vic. Scenic and easy.

Consider splitting a car with others arriving on the same flight to save on costs.

Good to KnowPractical Tips

Weather: El Brull sits at ~800m — August days are warm (24–30°C) but evenings cool down nicely. Bring a light layer.

Visa: Spain is Schengen. EU/EEA passports enter freely. Others check requirements for Spain before travelling.

Currency: Euro (€). Cards accepted widely; bring a little cash for smaller villages and markets.

Data: Consider an EU/Spain eSIM — Airalo or Holafly offer affordable plans. Coverage in Brull can be variable.

Language: Catalan and Spanish both spoken. English works fine in most places.
